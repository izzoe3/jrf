Section 1

What is the Job Request Form?

The Job Request Form (JRF) is how all QIU staff submit design, media, photography, video, and web requests to the Digital Communications team. It replaces the old paper form and email process.

Any time you need something created or produced by Digital Comms ‚Äî a poster, a social media post, event photography, a website update, a video ‚Äî you start here.

Important Do not email the Digital Comms team directly to make a new request. All new requests must go through this form so they can be tracked, approved, and assigned properly. Requests made by email may not be actioned.
Section 2

Before You Start

Have the following ready before filling in the form. The more prepared you are, the faster your request will be processed.

1
A realistic due date
We require a minimum of 10‚Äì14 working days to complete most requests. Merchandise, T-shirts, and COO-level approvals require additional time. Do not set a due date that is less than two weeks away unless you have already spoken to us.
2
All the details of your project
Think through your request carefully before filling in the form. The description field is the most important part ‚Äî it tells us everything we need to do the job right without having to go back and forth. See Section 3 for guidance on what to include.
3
Your supporting materials on Google Drive (if any)
If you have logos, photos, reference images, or any existing files to share ‚Äî upload them to your Google Drive first and have the link ready. See Section 7 for how to share a Drive link correctly.
Section 3

Step-by-Step: Submitting a Request

1
Confirm your details
When you log in with your QIU account, your name, email address, department, and HOD will be filled in automatically. Check that these are correct before continuing ‚Äî particularly your faculty or department, as this helps us route and prioritise your request correctly.
2
Set your dates
The Requested Date is today's date ‚Äî it is filled in automatically. The Final Due Date is when you need the completed work. Remember the minimum lead time is 10‚Äì14 working days.
3
Write your Job Purpose
This is a one or two sentence summary ‚Äî what is this for? For example: "Promotional poster for the Faculty of Health Sciences Annual Symposium on 15 March 2025." Keep it short and specific.
4
Select your request category
Choose the category that best describes your request, then select the specific type from the options that appear. If your request covers more than one type (e.g. a poster and a banner for the same event), select the primary one and describe the rest in the description field.

Tip ‚Äî use the formatting tools The description field has formatting tools at the top ‚Äî you can use bullet points, numbered lists, and bold text to structure your brief clearly.

7

Click Submit Request. Your HOD will immediately receive a notification to endorse your request. You will receive an acknowledgement email ‚Äî keep this email, it is your communication channel for this request. Do not delete it.

Section 5

Tracking Your Request

You can check the current status of all your submitted requests at any time by visiting My Requests in the navigation bar at the top of the page.

Each request card shows your reference number, the request type, due date, and current status. Click on any card to expand it and see the full progress tracker and your request details.

What the statuses mean
Awaiting HOD Approval ‚Äî Your request has been submitted. Your HOD has been notified and needs to endorse it before we can begin.

HOD Approved ‚Äî Your HOD has endorsed your request. It is in our team's queue.

In Progress ‚Äî Our team is actively working on your request.

Completed ‚Äî Your project is ready for collection.
If your status does not seem to be moving Check whether your HOD has approved the request ‚Äî this is the most common cause of delays. If it has been approved and you have not heard anything in a while, reply to your acknowledgement email thread to follow up with our team.
Section 6

How to Communicate With Our Team

All communication about your request happens in one place only ‚Äî the acknowledgement email you received when you submitted your request.

1
Find your acknowledgement email
Search your inbox for an email from digitalcomms@qiu.edu.my with your reference number in the subject line, for example: [QIU-2025-0001] Job Request ‚Äî Poster ¬∑ Faculty of Health Sciences
2
Reply to that email ‚Äî do not write a new one
If you want to add more information, share additional files, or ask about your request ‚Äî click Reply on that email. All replies go into the same conversation thread. Do not send a separate new email to Digital Comms ‚Äî it will not be linked to your request and may not be seen.
Why we do it this way By keeping all communication in one email thread, nothing gets lost. Every reply is automatically attached to your reference number. Our team can see the full conversation without hunting through separate emails. And you have a complete record of everything in one place.
Section 7

Sharing Supporting Materials

If you have files to share with us ‚Äî logos, photos, existing documents, reference images ‚Äî please use Google Drive. Do not attach large files to emails.

1
Upload your files to Google Drive
Open drive.google.com and create a new folder for your request (e.g. "QIU-2025-0001 Symposium Poster"). Upload all your files into this folder.
2
Set the correct sharing permission
Right-click the folder ‚Üí Share ‚Üí Get link ‚Üí change the access from "Restricted" to "Quest International University ‚Äî Anyone with the link can view." This is critical. If the folder is still set to Restricted, our team will see a "You need to request access" error and cannot open your files.
3
Copy the link and paste it into the form
Copy the shareable link and paste it into the Supporting Materials ‚Äî Google Drive Link field on the request form. If you forget before submitting, reply to your acknowledgement email with the Drive link.
Common mistake to avoid Many people share a link but forget to change the permission from "Restricted." Always double-check before submitting. You can test it by opening the link in a private/incognito browser window ‚Äî if it asks you to request access, the permission is still restricted.
Section 8

Frequently Asked Questions

How long will my request take?
Most requests take 10‚Äì14 working days from the point of HOD approval. Complex requests such as videos, booklets, or exhibition booth designs may take longer. Merchandise and T-shirts require COO approval first, which adds to the timeline. If you have a hard deadline, please flag it clearly in your description and ensure you are submitting with enough lead time.
My HOD hasn't approved yet ‚Äî what do I do?
We cannot begin work until your HOD endorses the request. Please follow up with them directly. They would have received an email from digitalcomms@qiu.edu.my asking them to approve. If they cannot find it, ask them to check their spam folder or contact them in person.
I forgot to include some information ‚Äî how do I add it?
Find the acknowledgement email you received from digitalcomms@qiu.edu.my and reply to it with the additional information. Do not submit a new request. Our team will see your reply in the same thread as your original request.
Can I submit one request for multiple items?
Yes ‚Äî if multiple items are for the same project or event, you can submit them in one request. Select the primary category and describe all the items clearly in the description field, including individual sizes, quantities, and specifications for each item. If the items are for completely different projects, please submit separate requests.
I need this urgently ‚Äî can I get it done faster?
Please submit your request as normal and clearly state the urgency and reason in the description field. Our team will review and contact you via the email thread to discuss whether an expedited timeline is possible. We cannot guarantee rushed requests, especially if our team already has a full schedule. Planning ahead is the best way to avoid this situation.
My status has been "In Progress" for a long time ‚Äî what should I do?
Reply to your acknowledgement email thread and ask for an update. Include your reference number in the message. Our team will respond and let you know the current status of your project.
I need to make changes to a request I already submitted ‚Äî what do I do?
Reply to your acknowledgement email thread as soon as possible with the changes you need. If work has not started yet, we can update the brief. If work has already started, changes may require additional time ‚Äî please communicate this early.
Who do I contact if something is not working on this platform?
Email digitalcomms@qiu.edu.my directly and describe the issue. Please include what page you were on and what you were trying to do.
